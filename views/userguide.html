User guide for contracts with arbitration

Every contract with arbitration has two sides of the contract: offerer and acceptor. One of them is a buyer, another one is seller. A third party in the form of arbiter can be called to resolve disputes. As a last resort, in some situations, an ArbStore moderator can step into play to compensate fees to dispute plaintiff, in case of arbiter desicion was wrong (from the plaintiff point of view) or the arbiter behavior was incorrect.

1. Pair with the person you want to enter into a contract in an Obyte wallet app by clicking "Add a new device" on Chat tab, then follow the instructions on your screen.

(OPTIONAL) You can attest your real name before starting the contract so your counterparty will see your attested real name during contract signing. Same works for the peer you going to step into agreement with, they can attest themselves too prior sending you their address.

Real Name attestation can be done via a Real Name Attestation bot found in a Bot Store (the second tab of your chat window). Add this bot and follow the instructions it sends to you.

(OPTIONAL) If you got attested, you can share your private profile to your peer so they can see your real name alongside the fact that you were attested.

2. After chat dialog opens with the corresponding peer, acceptor sends to an offerer his address by clicking "Insert my address".

3. Offerer then click on the received address and choose "Offer contract with arbitration".

4. Offerer then fills the form.

4.1. First, they select the roles for them (Buyer/Seller), implicitly setting the opposite role for the other contract party.

4.2. Then enter the amount that buyer is gonna pay to the seller for their work. You can also select the asset in which the payment should be made. A good candidate for the asset can be the stablecoin (OUSDv2), that you need to obtain first from https://ostable.org.

4.3. Then pick the arbiter from this ArbStore. You can filter them by their specializations and compare prices for their services. Enter the addres of picked arbiter in the field.

4.4. Now fill in the contract title and text. Be sure that you mention the names and roles of both persons in the contract text, as in real world contracts.

4.5. You can also optionally provide your contact info, so both the peer and the arbiter can contact you through any messengers / phone / email, etc.

When you done, click "Offer contract" button to send the the contract to your peer for signing.

5. Acceptor then receives this contract offer via chat, opens it and accepts the contract, optionally providing their contacts.

6. After accepting the contract, when offerer comes online, their wallet will post the unit into DAG with contract hash, as a public confirmation of an aggreement. Acceptor needs to sign this unit.

7. After unit was posted, buyer needs to top-up the contract with the amount specified in it by opening the contract in their wallet and clicking Pay button.

8. After contract was paid, the seller can start fulfilling his contract obligations. After the work is done and the buyer is satisfied with the results, they can release the funds from the contract to the seller by clicking "release funds" button in the contract view. Success, contract is now completed.

9. The seller side can cancel the contract at any time and return the funds back to the buyer by clicking the "Refund" button in their wallet.

10. In case if some controversy happens during the work process and peers can't come to an agreement by themselves, any party of the contract can raise the dispute and call the arbiter by clicking open dispute link.

11. After the dispute link was clicked, the Arbiter selected in the contract will ask the plaintiff to pay for his service first. The payment is done through ArbStore of the arbiter, the plaintiff will receive a payment request from the ArbStore in chat.

After the payment is sent, Arbiter can start resolving the dispute. During that, arbiter will pair with both contract parties to collect details and evidencies from both peers to come to a conclusion which party of the contract has the rights to claim the funds from the contract. Arbiter posts a specially crafted unit into DAG which allows the winning party to claim funds to his own address.

12. If arbiter closed the dispute in your favor, you will be notified about it in chat and can now claim funds from the contract by clicking "Claim funds" button in the contract view.

13. If any of the contract parties is not satisfied with the outcome of arbiter's actions, they can appeal to their decision through contract view. This action will invoke ArbStore moderator that will look into arbiter's decision and can either agree with them and close the appeal, or compensate the losing side some bonus for incorrect arbiter decision.
